About me

I finished my masters degree in commercial engineering writing a thesis about Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) applications for Small/ Medium sized Enterprises (SME). While writing my thesis, I became more and more interested in coding and decided to eventually code a small ERP application myself. This was back in 2012-13. I had been developing in Visual basic for applications (VBA) before, but this was the first application I created using a real IDE (Visual Studio 2010 back then). Ever since, I have been hooked on coding. So, after a short career as a supply chain advisor in Pittsburgh, USA, I decided to throw my life around and get a job as a programmer. I started as a SAP ABAP programmer at Accenture, Amsterdam in April 2013. Accenture sent me to Pune, India for 6 months. This was a wonderful experience, and connected me with some of the brightest coders. I didn't like ABAP that much as a coding language, so when I got back to the Netherlands, I made the switch to Microsoft Dynamics AX. X++ was interesting and got me into the curly-brace languages. I did DAX for about 6 months, but didn't really enjoy it either. It lacked the creative-artsy aspect. I quit my job at Accenture in May 2014 to spend more time on some personal projects, most of them in C#, javascript and php. Since then I have been immersed in the wonderful world of web development, studying and applying the scripting languages and frameworks.
